    About Me

    Hello (and as the Xhosa, Zulu, Sotho & Afrikaans’ would say: Molweni, Sanibona, Dumelang & Goeie dag). Welcome, I really appreciate you stopping by to take a look at my site. I hope you don’t mind if I tell you a little about myself. My name is Ziyanda, a name that means “increase in daughters” in Xhosa, which is something greatly celebrated in the Xhosa culture. I am a New York based full stack Web Developer who was born and raised in South Africa, then immigrated to the United States as an adult. I am a technology enthusiast. I love to code and design beautiful simple things, and I absolutely love what I do.

    I recently asked someone to describe me in a few words. They said, “You find a way. When you want to accomplish something, you set a course. No matter how long it takes, no matter the number of obstacles, you won’t stop until you get there.” Not to be morbid, but that is something I’d like to have carved on my tombstone. I am determined to get the most out of my God-given talents and every opportunity I have to let them shine. I pride myself on my hard work and my "go gene", because it's the only way to make certain that I am on my path and at my pace is to grind. Even in my personal creative interest endeavors, like film making, the finished product is the result of countless hours of work and critical decision-making that occur before, during, and after the actors arrive at set and scenes are shot. Those two gifts—a strong work ethic and a knack for figuring out the most effective way to envision a problem and implement solutions—are the thread running through the fibers of my life.
